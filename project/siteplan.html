Site Plan: Find Your Perfect Car

Site Name

Name: Find Your Perfect Car

Reasoning: I chose this name because it is action-oriented and clearly communicates the core purpose of the website. It immediately tells the visitor what value they will get out of the site.

Site Purpose

This website helps users find the car that best fits their needs, lifestyle, and budget. Visitors can compare different vehicles, explore features, and receive recommendations based on their specific preferences—such as family size, fuel efficiency, performance, and price range.

Scenarios

Here are three questions that represent the target audience visiting the site:

  • "What is the most fuel-efficient family SUV under $30,000?"
  • "How can I compare the safety features and trunk space of two different sedans side-by-side?"
  • "What are the best car recommendations for a daily commuter who values performance and tech features?"

Color Schema

The site uses the following color palette to establish a trustworthy, modern, and automotive feel:

  • Slate Blue (#546A7B): Used for primary headings, the header/footer backgrounds, and main navigation links. It gives a sense of security and reliability.
  • Cool Steel Gray (#9EA3B0): Used for secondary backgrounds, borders, wireframes, and accent sections. It pairs cleanly with the blue to represent a metallic/automotive theme.

Typography

I have selected two Google Fonts for this project:

  • Montserrat: Used for all headings (h1, h2, h3). This font is a modern geometric sans-serif that creates strong, recognizable branding.
  • Roboto: Used for all body paragraphs and lists. It is highly legible and clean, making it perfect for reading longer descriptions or comparing vehicle specifications.
